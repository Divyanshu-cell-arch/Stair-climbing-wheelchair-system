# TerraHopper: Design and Development of a Sustainable Stair-Climbing Wheelchair

## Project Overview

TerraHopper is an innovative stair-climbing wheelchair designed to improve mobility and independence for elderly and differently-abled individuals. The project focuses on developing an affordable, lightweight, and mechanically efficient alternative to existing stair-climbing wheelchairs, which are often expensive, bulky, and inaccessible.

The design integrates an innovative **sun-planet gear transmission**, **lever-actuated mode switching mechanism**, and **hydraulic disc braking system** to enable smooth movement on both flat surfaces and staircases while maintaining simplicity, safety, and ease of manufacturing.

---

## Problem Statement

Millions of elderly and differently abled individuals face mobility challenges due to inaccessible infrastructure such as stairs, uneven terrain, and the absence of ramps or elevators. Existing robotic stair-climbing wheelchairs are costly, heavy, and mechanically complex, limiting their adoption in low- and middle-income communities.

The objective of TerraHopper is to develop a cost-effective mechanical solution that restores mobility while remaining simple, reliable, and manufacturable.

## Mechanical Working Principle

The wheelchair utilizes a **sun-planet gear mechanism** integrated within the rear wheels.

- During normal operation, user input rotates the sun gear, transmitting power to the rear wheels for efficient propulsion.
- For stair climbing, a lever mechanism redirects power to the rotating planet carrier, allowing the wheel assembly to negotiate stairs and uneven terrain.
- A hydraulic disc brake mounted on the main shaft provides controlled braking and enhanced user safety.
